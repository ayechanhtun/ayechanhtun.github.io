About Me

Hi, my name is Aye Chan. Born and raised in sunny South Africa, I hadn't planned to travel any further than Cape Town for my university studies. And when the opportunity came for me to come to Canada, I was terrified. But I also knew I couldn't say no.

So, armed with my camera and my luggage, I travelled 15,000 kilometres to try this thing called Systems Design Engineering. They told me it was like learning a bit of everything, and I did like learning—everything.

I want to build solutions that will make a positive impact on the future of the world. I'm curious about anatomy, physiology, and enjoy learning about how brains work. Because of this, many of my projects involve things like biological signals measurement and analysis, applications in medical engineering, human factors design, and biomechanics modelling and simulation. I've even teamed up with three like-minded students to work on building a solution for surgical mitral valve repair.

It is my curiosity of the world that motivates me to learn new things, and it has helped me develop an analytical approach to problem-solving with an unceasing desire to apply and improve upon it. It's exciting to see where technology can take us in the next few years, but it's also important to stay mindful of who we might be leaving behind.

When I'm not working on something engineering-related, I like to work on my photography and my writing. I also recently took up Muay Thai and am enjoying the discipline that comes with repeatedly striking a heavily-padded opponent. I try to spend some of my spare time in the darkroom, too. There's just something about film and negatives, and the chemical baths—developer, stop, and fix—that I can't seem to let go of.

If you're working on a project that you think I could help with or if you want to pick my brain about something, don't hesitate to contact me. You can find me on social media and through email. Let's sit down and learn from each other.
